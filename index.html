What Is Astrology?

Fundamentally, astrology is the study of the relationship between the stars. All the same, the stars we refer to in astrology additionally include the sun, the planets and the moon. Astrology has been utilized through it's long history as a form of popular entertainment, a part of religion, and an integral part of our daily lives.

Astrology starts by mapping the planets, including the sun and the moon. The map is known as the astrological chart, and often an astrological chart is designed for someone when they are born. To determine the position of the sun, moon and planets for an astrological chart the astrologist needs to find the precise location of someone's birth, the precise moment in time, and and of course, the date.

The planets that feature on the astrological chart are the moon, the Sun, Venus, Saturn, Neptune, Jupiter, Mars Pluto, Uranus and Mercury. The earth is not considered as it is the place in the universe from which we view the rest of planets. The moon and sun are treated planets in astrology, which is difficult for some people to remember.

The astrological chart is comprised of of twelve houses, and each house represents a facet of life, like romance, career, family, etc. As there are only ten planets, each astrological chart has two vacant houses. These two houses correspond to facets of life which, for some reason, the powers of the cosmos do not feel you have to examine.

This is the first aspect of astrology examined on the astrological chart. After this, the positions of other planets are noted in relation to their effect on the sun sign. Last of all, the moon sign and position is studied in relation to the rest of the astrological chart.

The interpretation of an astrological birth chart as a whole will paint the picture of the original character of the individual. The birth chart found through astrology later in life might not seem to be accurate. This is because other astrological phenomena in conjunction with events in life change our character as time passes.

Astrology is the study of an astrological chart, as well as future predictions based on how other astrological phenomena may influence the original interpretations. The translation of the astrological chart is a major part of the art of astrology, and is essential for accurate predictions.

The utilization of astrology when making any predictions is seen as an art for a reason. Not only does the astrologist translate the birth chart and the present time astrological phenomena, but he or she must also take into account any astrological phenomena that may have changed the character of the individual over time.

Numerous important events in history can also be marked by astrology readings. The terror of 9/11 and the destruction of the twin towers was also awaited by those who study astrology. Of course, the birth of Christ was marked by astrological phenomena.

The 16 SANSKARS

There are 16 religious ceremonies known as Sanskars in Hindu culture. The Sanskars are performed for the Mental, physical, behavioral, and spiritual balance and progress of a individual. In these ceremonies, Agni (Fire) plays a really important part. Because Agni is one of the purest sorts of five major elements during this world, which we also called energy. These ceremonies create positive energy during a person and make him cooler and knowing to take new responsibilities and altered lifestyle, whether it's holy war or Sanyaas (self-exile for god) –holy war means a struggle against evil within and Sanyaas means detachment from lust, greed, hatred, and detachment from this materialistic world. The acknowledgment of this unique individualization process at every step of growth helps enrich the lifetime of the person. Sanskaras prepare one for the next stage of growth. These ceremonies are vital for a person's being to vary his mind and means also as fresh challenges.

1. Garbhadhana (Impregnation)

Conception acceptance ceremony. (After solemnizing wedding ceremony .) It is often offered as a symbolic pooja (prayer ceremony) with major ceremonies like"Naam Karan" and janeu Dharan and vivah (marriage).

2. Punsavana

The ceremony of graceful care of the embryo to the development of the fetus. Usually, it's undertaken during the second or third month of pregnancy. (it is often offered as a symbolic pooja(prayer ceremony) with major ceremonies like Naam Karan and janeu Dharan and vivah(marriage).

3. Simantonnayana

Inviting God's blessings for the event of the fetus to full birth. Usually, it's conducted from the fourth month of pregnancy, and, onwards, into the second, and therefore the trimester of pregnancy, almost like the women shower within the west. (It are often offered as symbolic pooja (prayer ceremony) with major ceremonies like Naam Karan and janeu Dharan and vivah(marriage).

4. Jatakarma

This is a crucial ceremony a while we call it a replacement birth of thoughts also. which is now forgotten by the common man. This is altogether accepting and identifying the newborn as an individual.

5. Namakarana (Naming a child)

The naming ceremony is usually on the eleventh day, usually from birth to twelve days. (Most important ceremony in Hindu culture. it decides the phonetics of the first letter of his or her name .this the first letter includes positions of planets at a birth time .it is the most scientific ritual and necessary for each person whether he's Hindu or non-Hindu.

The ceremony of invoking God's blessings to expose the newcomer to the outside world and to pray for the well being of the baby. 7. Annaprasana (first feeding of solid food)

Instead of liquid food, this ceremony is an Offering of solid food to the infant, usually undertaken from four to 6 months aged. (it is a kind of a thanks to the planet (earth as well as the universe) who will feed this human with air, water, fire, fields, and sky for the whole of his life.

8. Chudakarma (Mundan- first shaving of the head)

This is the most important ceremony in Hindu culture. It is a Self-awaking ceremony that gives new confidence to the child. (One year and onwards.)

9. Karnavedha (Piercing of the Ear)

This ceremony is known as “Kaan chedan pooja”. Here a human goes through a piercing of his/her ear and few prayers – poojas. that human also takes the blessings of elders. we can say it's a Self-esteem development ceremony that also has a great impact to possess control on a few veins (part of the nerve’s system) which are vital in our body. This ceremony is related to boys only. Adolescent age is that when human tries to explore each and every existing thing around him or self emerging thoughts. It reduces the fear of the outer world and makes a person strong for isolation and separation from his parents as he goes to high school or madrasa or Gurukul. It is also called Yajnopaveet Sanskar. It is associated with janeu Dharan ceremony. in this ceremony brother of the mother is the most important person in the whole pooja (ceremony). 11. Vedarambha

The education enrichment ceremony is undertaken when the child is five years of age. (It is often offered as symbolic pooja(prayer ceremony) with major ceremonies like Naam Karan and janeu Dharan and (vivah-marriage).

12. Samavartana

It is a welcoming ceremony to simply accept the grown-up as a professional individual. It is undertaken after completion of studies, to return home, and to organize for the subsequent stages of life. (it is often offered as symbolic pooja(prayer ceremony) with major ceremonies like Naam Karan and janeu Dharan and (vivah-marriage).

13. Vivaha(Marriage)

Vivah means - 2nd a part of our life and responsibilities. From here we take or accept the responsibilities and challenges of the new world all alone. Here we project ourselves a person who is mature and capable of doing everything in the future. It also provides new emotions and a sense of responsibility to a person. 14. Vanaprastha Ashram: - 3rd stage of life - (Detachment from the materialistic world).

After enjoying and fulfilling all the desires of Kama; By earning money, artha, by righteous means and by doing good deeds such as the Dharma, one can now move forward in life to win all remaining satisfaction. Vanaprastha Ashram is the name of the stage in life at which we expect our artificial interests to be removed from the demanding world. This period teaches us to save our time for God and poor people. this is usually done between the ages of fifty to seventy-five, and this stage is similar to the state of retirement planning. 15. Samnyasa Ashram (one might stay as a monk)

Samnyasa (one might stay as a monk) is selfless service to humanity, which is achieved by spiritual awakening. When one devotes his life to social exaltation without any personal gain, that person enters the ashram of Sanyas and therefore the ceremony confers the title of Sanyasi. A sannyasi usually wears an orange robe, which symbolizes selfless service to humanity. Orange can be a symbolic representation of blood. In other words, a sannyasi is willing to sacrifice his life for social advancement. This is usually done at the age of sixty or older. (frankly - this does not require any ceremony - when someone leaves all artificial things or lusts and burns all artificial emotions and strives for God and nature, then why bother with puja (prayer ceremony) / prayer at all).

This ceremony is the third ceremony in a person's life, which he cannot see or participate in. This is done by his or her beloved. This is the last ceremony we will perform when the body meets its true creators - means the five elements - fire (dynamic energy), water, air, sky (space) and earth (base). When the soul has left the mortal body, the remains are cremated. This ceremony is called Anti-Isthi. The elements of the body are consumed by fire, and therefore the remains are given over to nature. The body breaks down into five basic elements: earth, water, air, dynamic energy and space, and therefore the cycle begins again.
